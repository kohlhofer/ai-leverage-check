# The AI Leverage Check

A two-minute self-check on how much you're actually getting from AI.

Everyone thinks they're using AI fully. In practice everyone uses it differently, and most people rate themselves higher than they are, because frequency feels like leverage. It isn't.

The check looks at two things:

- **Capability** — how many of the things AI can do for you (connect to your systems, hold persistent context, run multi-stage workflows, close feedback loops, work semi-autonomously) you actually practise, and how deeply.
- **Impact** — whether your AI work has actually landed: shipped, used by others, a number you checked that moved.

Together they place you in one of four quadrants: Dabbler, Sharp operator, Output factory, or Force multiplier.

This is an opinionated self-check built from a point of view, not a validated measurement calibrated against data — a mirror to think with, not a score to defend. It's a single self-contained HTML file with no dependencies. Your answers are encoded in the share link; nothing is sent anywhere.
